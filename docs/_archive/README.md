# Archive

This directory contains completed process artifacts that are no longer part of
the student-facing curriculum. They are preserved for reference and historical
traceability but are not required reading.

## What Is Here

### specs/

Completed spec workstreams whose construction history is not in the student
reading path. The work they produced is live in the application — these are
the planning and sprint documents from that work.

| Workstream               | Sprints | What It Built                                                                               |
| ------------------------ | ------: | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| site-foundation          |       6 | Next.js scaffold, design system, content architecture, homepage shell, quality gates        |
| history-content-platform |      10 | Research workflow, content model, era map, 7 era packages, media pipeline, exemplar content |
| web-book-system          |       9 | Homepage grid, chronology section, reading model, scenes, footer, shared design system      |
| refactor/browser-polish  |       4 | DRY consolidation, browser CSS features, accessibility focus                                |

### research/

Internal process artifacts from the research workflow (search logs, operations
docs). The substantive research memos remain in `docs/_research/topics/`.

## Why These Were Archived

The student reading guide (`docs/foundation/student-reading-guide.md`)
recommends three workstreams for study: browser-enhancements, code-quality, and
documentation-foundation. Those remain in `docs/_specs/`. Everything here was
either superseded (browser-polish → browser-enhancements) or is construction
history that doesn't teach the orchestration method better than the recommended
examples.

## Can I Still Read These?

Yes. They are complete, accurate, and demonstrate the same spec/sprint/QA
workflow. They are just not the best starting examples for learning the method.
