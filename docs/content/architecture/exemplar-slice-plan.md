# Exemplar Slice Plan

## Selected Exemplar Era

The selected first exemplar era package is:

- `Computation, Information, And Field Formation (1936-1956)`

## Why This Slice Wins

- Sprint 0 research already supports it directly.
- It contains a clear chronological arc.
- It makes the origin story of AI legible without pretending AI begins from a
  single moment.
- It provides strong hooks for guide commentary, diagrams, and audio.
- It connects naturally to later modern concepts such as neural networks,
  information theory, and large language models.

## Core Narrative Throughline

The era package should teach that the modern AI field took shape when several
lines of work converged:

- formal models of computation
- logic implemented in machinery
- information and communication theory
- neuron-inspired computational abstraction
- institutional field formation at Dartmouth

The package should argue that Dartmouth named and consolidated the field, but
the intellectual runway was already in place.

## Initial People Units

- Alan Turing
  Reason: formal computation, machine intelligence framing, and the broader
  question of what machines can do.
- Claude Shannon
  Reason: bridge from logic to circuits, information theory, chess, and
  Dartmouth co-proposer.
- Warren McCulloch
  Reason: neural abstraction and the bridge between neurobiology and
  computation.
- Walter Pitts
  Reason: co-author of the 1943 threshold-neuron model and a key figure in the
  computational-neural origin story.
- John McCarthy
  Reason: field naming and Dartmouth framing.

## Initial Concept Units

- computability
- information theory
- threshold-neuron model
- cybernetics
- machine intelligence as a research agenda

## Initial Institution Units

- Bell Labs
- Dartmouth Summer Research Project on Artificial Intelligence

## Initial Debate Or Turning-Point Target

- Dartmouth as field formation, not single-point invention
  Reason: this is the clearest turning point in the slice and it prevents the
  package from collapsing into the misleading story that AI was simply created
  in one summer. It also gives the first exemplar package a natural place to
  connect historical narrative, guide commentary, and later concept teaching.

## Guide Commentary Targets

Use the `Why It Matters` pattern in at least these likely positions:

- after Turing's 1936 work
- after Shannon's 1938 relay-circuit bridge
- after the McCulloch-Pitts 1943 paper
- after Dartmouth's field-naming moment

Possible `Misconception Watch` placement:

- near Dartmouth, to correct the idea that AI was simply invented in one summer
