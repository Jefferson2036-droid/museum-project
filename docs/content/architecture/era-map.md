# Era Map

## Purpose

This era map defines the initial chronological spine for the AI history site.

The structure is intentionally broad enough to support navigation and teaching,
but specific enough to guide later content work.

## Decision On The Early Material

Sprint 0 left an open question about whether the early material should be one
single pre-Shannon-to-Dartmouth era or two adjacent eras.

The decision for this project is:

- use two adjacent early eras

Reason:

- it separates long-run intellectual and mechanical precursors from the more
  compressed 1936-1956 formation period
- it reduces cognitive overload for non-specialists
- it gives the first exemplar slice a sharper narrative center

## Initial Eras

### 1. Precursors To Machine Intelligence

- Date range: before 1936
- Role in the teaching journey:
  show that AI did not emerge from nowhere; it grew out of logic, calculation,
  automata, control, and questions about formal reasoning
- Core scope:
  Boole, Frege, Babbage, Lovelace, formal logic, mechanized calculation,
  feedback precursors, and the prehistory of computability

### 2. Computation, Information, And Field Formation

- Date range: 1936-1956
- Role in the teaching journey:
  establish the core origin story for modern AI by linking Turing, Shannon,
  McCulloch-Pitts, cybernetics, and Dartmouth
- Core scope:
  computability, relay logic, information theory, threshold-neuron models,
  wartime computing, machine-intelligence framing, and Dartmouth as a field-
  naming event
- Boundary rule:
  this era ends with Dartmouth as the field-formation moment; it covers the
  intellectual and institutional consolidation that made early AI legible as a
  named research agenda

### 3. Symbolic Optimism And Early AI Programs

- Date range: 1956-1969
- Role in the teaching journey:
  explain how the first generation of AI research translated the Dartmouth
  agenda into symbolic systems, search, theorem proving, and early optimism
- Core scope:
  symbolic AI, search, heuristics, early labs, and foundational optimism
- Boundary rule:
  this era begins after Dartmouth's naming and consolidation moment, when the
  newly articulated agenda is pursued through programs, labs, and symbolic AI
  research cultures

### 4. Knowledge Systems, Critique, And The First AI Winter

- Date range: 1970-1987
- Role in the teaching journey:
  show how ambitious claims encountered limits, critiques, funding pressure,
  and changing expectations
- Core scope:
  expert systems, knowledge engineering, critique of symbolic limits, winter,
  and institutional pressure

### 5. Statistical Learning And Network Revival

- Date range: 1988-2011
- Role in the teaching journey:
  explain the shift toward data-driven methods, probabilistic reasoning, and the
  renewed credibility of neural networks
- Core scope:
  statistical NLP, machine learning, backpropagation revival, larger datasets,
  and changing evaluation culture

### 6. Deep Learning Breakthroughs

- Date range: 2012-2019
- Role in the teaching journey:
  explain why deep learning became dominant and how modern performance leaps
  changed the field's public and technical expectations
- Core scope:
  large-scale neural networks, computer vision, speech, transformers, and the
  run-up to foundation models

### 7. Foundation Models And Generative AI

- Date range: 2020-2026
- Role in the teaching journey:
  connect the historical story to the systems contemporary students already know
  while keeping the repository's present horizon explicit rather than indefinite
- Core scope:
  large language models, multimodality, embeddings, public adoption, policy,
  labor, trust, and the renewed debate over intelligence and capability

## Navigation Rules

- The site should lead users through eras first.
- Every era should offer clear pivots to people, concepts, and institutions.
- Modern concepts should always point back to the earlier eras that made them
  possible.
- Adjacent eras may touch the same year at a turning point, but the transition
  event itself should be assigned to one primary era by the boundary rules
  rather than duplicated as the main focus of both.

## Exemplar Direction

The current leading candidate for the first live era package is:

- Era 2: Computation, Information, And Field Formation

Reason:

- the research base already exists
- the slice has strong human stories
- it connects naturally to guide commentary, diagrams, and audio planning
- it provides a clean bridge from history into modern AI concepts
