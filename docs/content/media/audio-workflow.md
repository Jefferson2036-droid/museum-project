# Audio Workflow

## Purpose

This document defines how audio-ready segment plans are created for the AI
history site.

Sprint 2 plans audio. It does not require fully produced narration.

## Core Rule

Audio should help a reader who benefits from hearing chronology, significance,
or contrast explained aloud.

It should not simply restate the full page in spoken form.

## What An Audio Plan Must Contain

Each audio plan should include:

- segment title
- linked era or turning point
- teaching purpose
- audience need
- target duration
- outline or script sketch
- pronunciation or pacing notes
- evidence basis
- next production step

## Audio Planning Rules

- prefer short segments with one clear interpretive job
- use audio where narrative pacing or conceptual contrast benefits from spoken
  emphasis
- align the segment with guide-commentary goals without duplicating a guide box
  line for line
- keep the plan historically specific and evidence-based

## Exemplar Audio Direction

The first required exemplar plan is:

- `dartmouth-origin-audio-plan.md`

It should inherit the existing memo idea: Dartmouth named AI, but did not
create every idea that made the field possible.

## Production Boundary

- Sprint 2 records the plan and readiness conditions
- later sprints may draft, generate, or produce the audio asset itself
