## One-Sentence Thesis

After Dartmouth named the field, early AI researchers pursued intelligence as
symbol manipulation, using search, heuristics, theorem proving, and early lab
cultures to turn a new agenda into working programs.

## Why This Era Matters

- it shows that post-Dartmouth AI produced real methods and not just bold
  promises
- it teaches why symbolic AI looked like a plausible path toward intelligence
- it builds the conceptual bridge to later debates over scaling, knowledge, and
  learning

## Core Narrative

Era 3 begins when Dartmouth stops being a naming moment and becomes a research
program. The key shift is practical: symbolic AI starts asking how a machine can
search through possibilities, use heuristics to narrow those possibilities, and
prove or plan over explicit symbolic structures. Logic Theorist and GPS make the
optimism legible because they show that reasoning can be operationalized in
programs rather than discussed only in theory.

The era also needs institutional texture. LISP, MIT's AI Lab culture, SRI's
robotics work, and DARPA-backed research programs turn a concept into a research
ecosystem. Shakey and STRIPS matter because they move symbolic AI beyond theorem
proving into planning and action. The best teaching frame is therefore not
"researchers believed too much too early" but "researchers built powerful early
methods whose limits were not yet fully visible."
