# Eliezer Yudkowsky

## Name

- name: Eliezer Yudkowsky
- lifespan or active period: active from the 2000s through the modern AI era

## Role In AI History

Yudkowsky is a key AI-safety lineage figure because he helped popularize the
arguments that very capable AI systems may pursue goals misaligned with human
values even without explicit malice.

## Why This Person Matters

- he gives Era 7 a clear link to the pre-frontier-model alignment discourse that
  shaped current public safety language
- he helps explain where ideas such as orthogonality, instrumental convergence,
  corrigibility difficulty, and the paperclip thought experiment became durable
  teaching tools
- he keeps the modern debate from collapsing into company statements alone by
  representing an external, high-stakes safety tradition

## Major Contributions In Plain Language

- wrote influential AI-risk and alignment arguments aimed at showing why powerful
  optimizers might be dangerous even when they are not designed to hate humans
- helped make the paperclip-maximizer thought experiment a standard way to teach
  alien goals and instrumental convergence
- remained a visible critic of the claim that raw model capability progress is
  being matched by equally strong understanding or control

## Tensions, Limitations, Or Misconceptions

- Yudkowsky should not be presented as a builder of today's frontier models;
  his historical importance here is as a safety and alignment thinker whose
  arguments shaped how many later readers interpret the risks of advanced AI
