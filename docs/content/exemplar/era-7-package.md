## One-Sentence Thesis

AI's current era begins when transformer-based systems become reusable
foundation models, large language models move into public life, multimodal and
embedding-driven infrastructure spreads, and debates over trust, labor,
governance, and capability become inseparable from deployment.

## Why This Era Matters

- it explains why contemporary AI feels different from the deep-learning era
  that came just before it
- it teaches that the modern shift is not only bigger models but also broader
  reuse, public interfaces, and institution-level deployment choices
- it gives students a clear present horizon of 2026 instead of an indefinite
  "now"

## Core Narrative

Era 7 should be taught as the period when AI becomes both widely visible and
deeply infrastructural. The key change from Era 6 is not simply that
transformers keep improving. The stronger historical claim is that large-scale
pretrained models become reusable bases for many downstream tasks, which turns a
research architecture into a general platform strategy.

That is why foundation models matter. A single model family can support
chatbots, coding tools, search assistance, summarization, retrieval pipelines,
image and audio systems, and other applications without starting from scratch
for every task. Large language models are the most visible version of this
shift, especially after public deployment makes conversational AI a daily
experience for students, workers, and institutions.

The era also needs to be broader than chat. Vector embeddings and latent-space
reasoning become part of the modern AI stack because retrieval, similarity, and
representation now matter across search, recommendation, semantic indexing, and
generative systems. Multimodality extends the same historical story beyond text,
showing that modern models increasingly connect language, images, audio, video,
and action in one technical landscape.

This period should not be written as a smooth triumph. Public deployment brings
stronger capability claims, but also sharper arguments about accuracy, labor,
education, bias, governance, safety, and who gets to decide when systems are
ready to use. In this repository, Era 7 reaches the current project horizon of
2026 rather than pretending the present is unbounded.
