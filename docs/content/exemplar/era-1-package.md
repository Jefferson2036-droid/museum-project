# Era 1 Package

## Title

- title: Precursors To Machine Intelligence
- date range: before 1936
- live route: `/eras/precursors-to-machine-intelligence`

## One-Sentence Thesis

Before AI became a named field, formal logic, mechanized calculation, and the
idea that symbolic procedures might be carried out by machines built the
intellectual runway that Era 2 later compresses into modern field formation.

## Why This Era Matters

- it prevents the site from treating AI as if it appeared suddenly in the 1940s
  and 1950s
- it gives students a clear prehistory for later computation and machine
  intelligence claims
- it makes the transition into Turing and Shannon feel earned rather than abrupt

## Core Narrative

Era 1 gathers the long-run precursors that make machine intelligence thinkable
before modern electronics, wartime computing, or Dartmouth enter the story.
Boole matters because formal logic turns reasoning into symbolic structure.
Babbage matters because mechanized calculation stops being merely a philosophical
fantasy and becomes a serious engineering idea. Lovelace matters because she
helps make clear that machines can follow symbolic procedures while still raising
hard questions about whether execution is the same thing as thought.

The era should not be taught as AI before AI. It should be taught as a careful
runway: the place where symbolic procedure, calculation, and machine design are
made legible enough that Era 2 can ask sharper questions about computation and
intelligence.
