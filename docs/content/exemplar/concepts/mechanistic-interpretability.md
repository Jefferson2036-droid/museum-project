# Mechanistic Interpretability

## Title

- title: Mechanistic interpretability

## Plain-Language Definition

Mechanistic interpretability is the effort to reverse engineer neural networks,
especially transformers, into human-understandable circuits or computational
parts rather than treating them only as black boxes judged by outputs.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It gives Era 7 a precise way to teach why modern LLMs are powerful without being
fully understood. That gap matters historically because deployment, safety, and
reliability debates now unfold faster than mechanistic understanding does.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- mechanistic interpretability becomes especially visible from the early 2020s
  onward as frontier-model researchers try to explain transformer circuits,
  in-context learning behavior, feature superposition, and safety-relevant
  internal structure

## Modern Relevance

The concept helps explain why many researchers say we still do not fully
understand how LLMs work internally. We can evaluate behavior and deploy useful
systems, but tracing the internal mechanisms behind those behaviors remains only
partly solved.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- interpretability is not the same thing as asking a model to explain itself;
  the stronger historical project is to understand the internal mechanisms that
  actually produce model behavior
