# Transformers

## Title

- title: Transformers

## Plain-Language Definition

Transformers are neural network architectures built around attention mechanisms,
allowing models to weigh relationships across a sequence without depending on
older recurrent processing step by step.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

They mark the late-Era-6 turning point that leads directly into foundation
models, large language models, and modern embedding-heavy AI systems.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- the 2017 Attention Is All You Need paper is the key turning point for this
  concept in the current release

## Modern Relevance

Transformers are the immediate historical runway into large language models,
modern embeddings, multimodality, and foundation-model scaling.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- transformers are not important just because they are newer; they matter because
  they changed sequence modeling, scaling, and later language-model capability in
  a historically decisive way
