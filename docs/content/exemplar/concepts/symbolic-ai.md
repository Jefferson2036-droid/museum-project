# Symbolic AI

## Title

- title: Symbolic AI

## Plain-Language Definition

Symbolic AI treats intelligence as the manipulation of explicit symbols,
representations, and rules rather than as pattern learning from raw data alone.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It is the central idea of the first post-Dartmouth research era, and it explains
why theorem proving, planning, and expert systems all felt like parts of one
coherent project.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- symbolic AI becomes the dominant early framing after Dartmouth, especially in
  work on reasoning, search, planning, and knowledge representation during the
  late 1950s and 1960s

## Modern Relevance

It still matters when explaining planning, formal verification, logic systems,
knowledge graphs, tool use, and why later statistical systems were seen as both
an alternative to and a complement for hand-built symbolic methods.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- symbolic AI should not be taught as mere fantasy or failure; it produced real
  techniques even where its broader ambitions did not scale as hoped
