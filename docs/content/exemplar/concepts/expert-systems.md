# Expert Systems

## Title

- title: Expert systems

## Plain-Language Definition

Expert systems are AI programs that encode specialist knowledge and rules so a
computer can make recommendations or inferences in a narrow domain.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

They are the strongest evidence in Era 4 that symbolic AI could produce useful,
high-value applications even while broader claims about general intelligence were
under pressure.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- expert systems become prominent from the late 1960s through the 1970s and
  early 1980s in systems such as DENDRAL, MYCIN, and XCON

## Modern Relevance

They remain a useful historical comparison for rule-based systems, decision
support, and present-day efforts to combine learned models with explicit
knowledge and tools.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- expert systems were not fake AI; they were one of the clearest demonstrations
  that symbolic methods could work when the domain and knowledge representation
  were tightly constrained
