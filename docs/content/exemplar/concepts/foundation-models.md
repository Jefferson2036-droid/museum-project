# Foundation Models

## Title

- title: Foundation models

## Plain-Language Definition

Foundation models are large pretrained models that can be adapted or prompted
for many downstream tasks rather than built fresh for each one.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It names the shift from impressive single-task deep-learning systems to reusable
general model bases that reshape products, research agendas, and institutional
power after 2020.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- foundation models emerge as the central framing of the 2020s when very large
  pretrained transformer systems begin supporting many tasks through prompting,
  adaptation, retrieval, and fine-tuning instead of narrow task-specific design

## Modern Relevance

Foundation models sit behind modern assistants, coding tools, retrieval stacks,
multimodal systems, and many AI products that reuse one underlying model family
across tasks.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- a foundation model is not just any large model; historically the term matters
  because one pretrained base can support many later applications and therefore
  changes deployment and governance questions
