# Machine Intelligence As Research Agenda

## Title

- title: Machine Intelligence As Research Agenda

## Plain-Language Definition

This concept marks the shift from isolated technical ideas toward an organized
effort to study whether machines can perform intelligent tasks.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It gives the exemplar slice a way to connect Turing's question, Shannon's
engineering imagination, and McCarthy's field-naming work.

It also connects the earlier precursor debate about procedure and symbolic
machinery to the later decision to organize machine intelligence as a named
research field.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- the concept builds across the late 1930s to mid-1950s and is consolidated at
  Dartmouth

## Cross-Era Teaching Note

Era 1 helps students see that symbolic procedure and machine capability were
already being debated before AI became a named agenda. Era 2 then narrows that
runway into explicit questions about machine intelligence and field formation.

## Modern Relevance

It helps explain why contemporary AI is both a technical domain and an ongoing
research program shaped by institutional choices.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- a research agenda is not the same as a solved theory of intelligence
