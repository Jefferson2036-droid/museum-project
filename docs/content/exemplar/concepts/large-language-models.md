# Large Language Models

## Title

- title: Large language models

## Plain-Language Definition

Large language models are transformer-based systems trained on very large text
corpora so they can predict, generate, and transform language across many kinds
of tasks.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

They are the most visible public form of the foundation-model era and the main
reason many non-specialists now encounter AI through conversation, writing,
coding, and search assistance.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- large language models become historically decisive after 2020, when scaled
  transformer systems such as GPT-3 show broad few-shot behavior and later
  conversational deployment turns them into everyday public interfaces

## Modern Relevance

LLMs power chat interfaces, search assistance, writing tools, coding support,
agent systems, and many of the public debates about modern AI capability.

They also make a harder modern teaching problem visible: researchers can build
and deploy highly capable systems while still lacking a deep mechanistic account
of how those systems implement many of their internal behaviors.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- an LLM is not historically important simply because it can chat; it matters
  because transformer scaling, broad pretraining, and public deployment changed
  how AI systems are built, reused, and socially understood
- the fact that an LLM can explain its own answer does not mean researchers
  already understand the internal mechanism that produced it
