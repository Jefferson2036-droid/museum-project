# Vector Embeddings

## Title

- title: Vector embeddings

## Plain-Language Definition

Vector embeddings are dense learned numerical representations that place words,
documents, images, or other items into a space where similar things tend to end
up near one another.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

They connect deep representation learning to modern AI infrastructure by making
similarity, retrieval, recommendation, and semantic search practical at scale.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- embeddings become central to the modern AI stack when learned dense
  representations replace many sparse hand-built encodings and later support
  retrieval and semantic similarity across foundation-model systems

## Modern Relevance

Embeddings help modern systems rank similar documents, retrieve relevant
context, cluster concepts, and connect user queries to stored knowledge without
depending only on exact keyword overlap.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- embeddings are not magical hidden meanings; they are learned vector
  representations that make useful relationships easier for models and search
  systems to compute
