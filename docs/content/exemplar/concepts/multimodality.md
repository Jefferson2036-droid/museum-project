# Multimodality

## Title

- title: Multimodality

## Plain-Language Definition

Multimodality is the ability of AI systems to work across more than one kind of
input or output, such as text, images, audio, video, or action.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It shows that the modern AI era is no longer only a language story; the same
foundation-model logic increasingly connects several media types in one system
family.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- multimodality becomes a defining current-era concept when foundation models
  begin linking text with images, audio, video, and action rather than treating
  each medium as a separate historical silo

## Modern Relevance

Multimodality helps explain why modern AI products increasingly combine seeing,
speaking, writing, hearing, and acting inside one interface or model family.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- multimodality does not mean that all AI modalities have fused into one solved
  intelligence problem; it means systems now connect several media types much
  more directly than earlier task-specific pipelines did
