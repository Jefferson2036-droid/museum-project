# AI Winter

## Title

- title: AI winter

## Plain-Language Definition

An AI winter is a period when confidence, funding, and public expectations fall
because the field's promises appear to outrun its practical results.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It gives the chronology a way to teach disappointment as a structured historical
event rather than as a vague story of failure.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- the first AI winter becomes legible in the 1970s and early 1980s as symbolic
  ambitions face critique, scaling problems, and tighter institutional support

## Modern Relevance

The term still shapes public debate whenever AI hype accelerates faster than the
field's ability to deliver robust systems in the real world.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- the first AI winter was not caused by one villain, one report, or one failed
  system; it came from a mix of technical limits, institutional pressure, and
  exaggerated timelines
