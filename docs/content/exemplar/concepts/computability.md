# Computability

## Title

- title: Computability

## Plain-Language Definition

Computability asks what kinds of procedures a machine can carry out in a precise
and repeatable way.

## Why The Concept Matters To AI History

It gives the exemplar slice a rigorous starting point for thinking about what
machines can do before anyone tries to claim they can think.

## Historical Emergence Or Turning Point

- Turing's 1936 work makes computation central to the era's origin story, but
  the precursor runway includes earlier formal logic and mechanized calculation

## Cross-Era Teaching Note

This concept should be taught as a bridge. Era 1 makes symbolic procedure and
mechanized calculation thinkable; Era 2 makes those questions precise enough to
anchor machine computation directly.

## Modern Relevance

Modern AI systems still depend on the assumption that intelligence claims must
eventually cash out in computable procedures and machine operations.

## Common Misconception To Avoid

- computability is not the same as intelligence, but it sets the stage for later
  AI claims
