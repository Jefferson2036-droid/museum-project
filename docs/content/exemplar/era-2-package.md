# Era 2 Package

## Title

- title: Computation, Information, And Field Formation
- date range: 1936-1956
- live route: `/eras/computation-information-field-formation`

## One-Sentence Thesis

Modern AI became thinkable when formal computation, logic in machinery,
information theory, and neuron-inspired abstraction converged before Dartmouth
named the field.

## Why This Era Matters

- it gives the site a coherent origin story without pretending AI began in a
  single instant
- it connects foundational figures to concepts that still shape current AI
- it ends with Dartmouth as a field-formation moment rather than a myth of
  one-summer invention

## Core Narrative

The exemplar slice starts with Turing's 1936 model of computation, which gives
the story a rigorous account of what a machine can compute in principle. It
then moves through Shannon's relay-circuit work and information theory, which
show how logic and communication become machine-relevant in engineering
practice. McCulloch and Pitts bring in a neuron-inspired abstraction that keeps
the eventual AI story connected to both biology and logic.

The era closes at Dartmouth, where John McCarthy and collaborators name and
consolidate a research agenda that earlier work had already made intellectually
possible. The package should therefore treat Dartmouth as the closing turning
point of Era 2, not as a shortcut past the decades of groundwork that precede
it.
