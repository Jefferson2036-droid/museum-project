# Attention Is All You Need And The Transformer Shift

## Title

- title: Attention Is All You Need and the transformer shift

## Core Question Or Conflict

What changed when sequence models stopped depending mainly on recurrence and
instead centered attention as the main architectural principle?

## Why It Matters

This turning point explains why late-Era-6 AI becomes the direct runway into
foundation models. It marks a specific architectural change, not just more hype
around already-familiar neural networks.

## Positions Or Pressures Involved

- recurrent and convolutional sequence models had already produced strong
  results, but they carried training and scaling limits
- attention mechanisms had proven useful, but not yet as the entire core of the
  architecture
- the transformer reframed the problem by making attention, parallelization, and
  long-range sequence relationships central to the design

## Outcome Or Continuing Uncertainty

The immediate outcome is that transformers quickly become a dominant language and
sequence-modeling architecture. The longer-term consequence, which belongs more
fully to Era 7, is that this same architectural shift enables foundation models,
large language models, and a much broader embeddings story.
